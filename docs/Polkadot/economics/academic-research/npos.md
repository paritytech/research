title: Approval-Based Committee Voting in Practice
---

| Published as Proceeding of AAAI Conference on AI   | 06.10.2025 | [AAAI](https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/AAAI/article/view/28807) / [ARXIV](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.11408) |


Web3 Foundation research has provided the first large-scale data collection of real-world approval-based committee elections. These elections, conducted on the Polkadot blockchain, are part of the Nominated Proof-of-Stake mechanism. They contain around one thousand candidates and tens of thousands of (weighted) voters each. The in-depth study included application-relevant questions, as well as a quantitative and qualitative analysis of the outcomes returned by different voting rules. Apart from considering proportionality measures standard in the multiwinner voting literature, attention was paid to less-studied measures of overrepresentation, as these are closely related to the security of the Polkadot network. Another aspect of the analysis focused on how different design decisions, such as committee size, can affect the examined measures.
