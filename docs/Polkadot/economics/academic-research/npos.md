title: Approval-Based Committee Voting in Practice

| Published as Proceeding of AAAI Conference on AI   | 06.10.2025 | [AAAI](https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/AAAI/article/view/28807) / [ARXIV](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.11408) |


Parity Research has provided the first large-scale data collection of real-world approval-based committee elections. These elections, conducted on the Polkadot blockchain, are part of the Nominated Proof-of-Stake mechanism. Each election contains around one thousand candidates and tens of thousands of (weighted) voters each. The in-depth study includes application-relevant questions, as well as quantitative and qualitative analyses of the outcomes returned by different voting rules. In addition to considering proportionality measures standard in the multiwinner voting literature, the study also examines less-studied measures of overrepresentation, as these are closely related to the security of the Polkadot network. Another aspect of the analysis focuses on how different design decisions, such as committee size, can affect the measures examined.
